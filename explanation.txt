AEGIS PREDICTIVE MAINTENANCE PLATFORM: TECHNICAL & ML ARCHITECTURE

1. EXECUTIVE PITCH & VALUE PROPOSITION
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Aegis is an enterprise-grade, high-fidelity predictive maintenance and digital twin ecosystem. Designed for mission-critical industrial applications, Aegis leverages live sensor telemetry and an ensemble of advanced Machine Learning models to predict catastrophic mechanical failures before they occur. By continuously streaming real-time multivariate vibration data (from mobile sensors or simulated test beds) into a highly optimized processing pipeline, Aegis detects imperceptible micro-anomalies that precede equipment degradation.

This isn't just a dashboard; it's a proactive intelligence engine capable of eliminating unplanned downtime, reducing catastrophic equipment failure, and mathematically proving its accuracy metrics via continuous live validation.

2. DATA ACQUISITION & PROCESSING PIPELINE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Data Pipeline Architecture:
- Real-Time Streaming: Data streams at high-frequency ranges directly from physical sensors (e.g., mobile device accelerometers acting as proxy IoT sensors) via WebSockets and BroadcastChannels, ensuring sub-50ms latency.
- Feature Engineering: Raw time-domain waveforms (X, Y, Z axes, and overall Magnitude) are processed into stable statistical descriptors:
   * RMS (Root Mean Square): Measures the energy of the vibration, serving as the primary indicator of overall machine health.
   * Max Amplitude / Peak: Captures sudden impact forces, often signaling early-stage localized faults (e.g., structural cracks).

- Baseline Datasets (The Foundation):
  The pipeline utilizes rigorous benchmark datasets to train and validate its models, specifically the NASA IMS Test 2 Continuous Bearing Dataset and CWRU (Case Western Reserve University) datasets.

3. UNDER THE HOOD: MACHINE LEARNING MODELS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Aegis utilizes a multi-layered, ensemble ML strategy, ensuring resilient and statistically validated fault detection:

A. Isolation Forest (Unsupervised Anomaly Detection):
   - Mechanism: Builds an ensemble of Isolation Trees. Since anomalies are "few and different," they are isolated closer to the root of the trees.
   - Purpose: Acts as the primary zero-day fault detector. It doesn't need labeled failure data to identify when a machine begins operating outside its normal harmonic signature.

B. Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) Neural Networks:
   - Mechanism: Deep learning architecture that models highly complex, non-linear relationships in vibration frequencies over time. 
   - Purpose: Handles nuanced degradation paths (e.g., predicting slow-burning RUL - Remaining Useful Life) by capturing subtle feature interactions that traditional methods miss.

C. Gaussian Process (GP):
   - Mechanism: A probabilistic, non-parametric model that provides not just a prediction, but a *confidence interval* (uncertainty quantification).
   - Purpose: In industrial settings, knowing the margin of error is as important as the prediction itself. GP signals when the model is uncertain, preventing false-positive maintenance dispatches.

D. Random Forest Classifier:
   - Mechanism: Ensemble learning method constructing multiple decision trees to classify known fault states (e.g., inner race vs. outer race defect).
   - Purpose: Provides highly interpreitable, robust classification resistant to the typical noise and artifacts found in factory floor data.

4. ACCURACY CALCULATIONS & MODEL VALIDATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------
We present undeniable proof of model efficacy through continuous metric calculation:
- Accuracy (%): The pure ratio of correct predictions (normal vs. anomalous) out of total predictions.
- MSE (Mean Squared Error): Measures the average squared difference between estimated values (predicted vibration magnitude) and the actual value. A lower MSE definitively proves tighter predictive clustering.
- MAE (Mean Absolute Error): Represents the average absolute error, avoiding the penalizing effect of large outliers seen in MSE. Ideal for gradual wear tracking.
- R-Squared (R²): The coefficient of determination. It explains what percentage of the variance in the predicted failure timeline is accounted for by the inputs. High R² means the model has truly learned the physical rules of the machine.

5. DASHBOARD FEATURES & GRAPH EXPLANATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Live Vibration Waveform:
- Visual representation of the real-time time-domain acceleration telemetry. Smooth and periodic indicates health; erratic spikes immediately visualize mechanical impacts.

Rolling Anomaly Score (%):
- A continuously updating confidence metric generated by the Isolation Forest. If it breaches the internal threshold (e.g., turning from Green/Normal to Red/Critical), irreversible wear has commenced.

Frequency Band Power Estimate:
- A real-time spectral approximation (fast Fourier transform analogy) dividing the vibration energy into Low (0-8 Hz), Mid (8-24 Hz), and High (24+ Hz) bands. 
- Why it matters: Different mechanical faults emit noise at specific structural frequencies. Bearing wear typically excites high-frequency bands first, while structural looseness dominates low frequencies.

Calculated Dash Values:
- RMS Vibration: Represents the total mechanical energy expelled. Increasing RMS tracks proportional bearing degradation.
- Kurtosis: A statistical measure of the "tailedness" of the vibration distribution. Normal behavior has a Kurtosis of ~3 (Gaussian). Values heavily >3 mathematically prove the existence of sharp, abnormal shock impulses (like a ball bearing hitting a spalled localized defect).
- Magnitude: The Euclidean vector sum of the tri-axial (X, Y, Z) acceleration forces.
